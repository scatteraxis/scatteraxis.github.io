What are fighting games?

Fighting games are a type of action video game where the core of the gameplay features combat between two or more players fighting each other on screen. This gameplay style was widely popularized by the commercial success of Capcom's Street Fighter, and most iconically its sequel and updates to Street Fighter II.

There are various different types of fighting games:

  • "Footsies" fighters: Fighting games that stick to a more grounded and slower paced approach to gameplay. Examples include the Street Fighter series and Mortal Kombat series.
  • "Anime" fighters: A term that has been used to refer to fighting games that contain the traits of both an "anime" visual or graphical style, and also a heavy focus on having many universal or character-specific mechanics. This design philosophy dates back to Capcom's Darkstalkers series of games, however it was massively popularized by Arc System Works' Guilty Gear franchise soon after.
  • "3D" fighters: Fighting games that make use of 3D space for the fighters instead of the much more common 2D space. Many of these games forgo certain special mechanics from other genres of fighting games and instead aim for a much more "intense" or "realistic" fighting system based on real-world martial arts and fighting technique.

About Me

My name is Agyei, and I am a 22 year old born and raised in Charlotte, NC, but my parents came to this country from Ghana. I knew fighting games existed for a while as a child, but I never really dived into one until I stumbled upon the arcade fighting game JoJo's Bizarre Adventure: Heritage for the Future, which was a licensed fighting game based on my favorite manga/anime series (at the time) of the same name. This was the first fighting game I ever legitimately tried to get good at, and it kickstarted my love for the genre as a whole, though ironically enough I kind of now hate most of the first few fighting games I tried to learn.

To this day I still play fighting games very often if not every day. I like to spend a lot of my free time researching more obscure games in the genre such as various licensed fighting games that severely underdocumented and underrepresented.

Where?

Where do I play fighting games?

You can play most fighting games from the comfort of your room thanks to the Internet, however most fighting game players such as myself like to meet up with friends and other players in real life as they are much better enjoyed socially rather than alone or against faceless opponents. At UNCC, I meet up with my friends just about every day in a common area in the Student Union ("Norm's Loft") to run games.

We also host one club event a month for any fighting game players in the area to come get some games, meet people, and have fun.

Why?

Why do I play fighting games?

I play fighting games because I believe them to be one of the most technically and mechanically interesting genres of games out there, which is very entertaining to me. Many of these games have been played for years and years since they released with absolutely no updates because their core gameplay is so well thought out and facilitates players sticking around to explore the systems for a very, very long time. There is always something interesting to pick out and research in any fighting game, and also so many to go through with. No two fighting games are ever the same, with most of them being only loosely comparable.

I'm also very invested in the community aspect of fighting games. The communities for many of these games are all purely in it for the love of the game(s) for what they are and the love of self-improvement in their play. Compared to modern games, it's very refreshing seeing people enjoy a game for what it is over "what it could become" or "what it could have been".

How?

How can I get started playing fighting games?

It's as easy to start playing as any other game genre. Find a game that you think looks cool, and you get it and you play it. Most games have a tutorial and some singleplayer modes for you to play, and once you feel like you're adept with them you can then try playing the games versus people online or in person. There are also fighting games on every game system, so they are fairly accessible as well.

There are also many websites that host information repositories of how to play many games, and also important technical data within the games such as frame data and hitboxes.
